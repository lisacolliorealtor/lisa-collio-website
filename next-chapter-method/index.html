The Next Chapter Method™

Real estate decisions start with life — not with houses.

The Next Chapter Method™ is the way Lisa Collio works with every buyer and seller in Goshen and Elkhart, Indiana: understand the season of life you're in first, and let the real estate decision serve it — never the other way around.

Here's something most people already know, even if no one says it out loud: nobody wakes up one morning wanting to fill out mortgage paperwork or host showings. People reach out to a real estate agent because life changed — or because they're ready for it to change. A wedding. A new job. A house that got too small, or too big. A parent who needs more help than before. A fresh start nobody planned for.

The house is rarely the reason. It's the response.

That's why Lisa doesn't start conversations with square footage or price ranges. She starts with a simpler question: what chapter of life are you living — and how can your next move support it?

The Five Life Chapters

Over years of serving families across Goshen and Elkhart, a pattern shows up again and again. Nearly every client's story fits one of five chapters — not because people are the same, but because life tends to unfold in recognizable seasons.

  1. Starting Out

    The first place of your own. Building something new — a career, a marriage, a future.

    The question underneath: "Who am I becoming?"

  2. Growing

    More space, new goals, life expanding.

    The question underneath: "How do I build the life I imagine?"

  3. Changing Direction

    A relocation, a divorce, a loss, an unexpected turn.

    The question underneath: "How do I move forward after life changes?"

  4. Simplifying

    Less house, more freedom. Designing the years ahead on purpose.

    The question underneath: "How do I intentionally design what comes next?"

  5. Legacy

    Protecting what you've built and the people you love.

    The question underneath: "How do I take care of the people who matter most?"

Whatever chapter you're in, the real estate decision looks different — because the life behind it is different. A first-time purchase and a downsizing sale might involve the same kind of house on the same street in Goshen. They are not the same decision.

The Smart Move Framework™

Understanding your chapter is the starting point. The Smart Move Framework™ is how Lisa turns that understanding into a plan — six steps, in the same order, every time:

  1. 1

    Discover

    Understand the person before discussing the property. What matters most to you? What are you protecting? What's driving the timing?

  2. 2

    Educate

    Replace uncertainty with clarity. Options explained, questions answered, no pressure — because confident decisions come from understanding, not urgency.

  3. 3

    Unlock

    Build the strategy that aligns the real estate decision with your larger goals. The right move isn't always the obvious one.

  4. 4

    Execute

    Handle the details — pricing, marketing, negotiation, paperwork — with professionalism and clear communication at every step.

  5. 5

    Transition

    Support life after the transaction: trusted local professionals, community resources, help settling into the next chapter, not just the next address.

  6. 6

    Celebrate

    The closing isn't the finish line. The first dinner in the new kitchen, the first quiet morning, the fresh start — that's what the move was for.

What's a "Smart Move"?

Not necessarily the highest price, the fastest sale, or the trendiest neighborhood.

A Smart Move is the housing decision that best supports the life you want to build, protect, or leave behind. Sometimes that's buying. Sometimes it's selling. Sometimes it's waiting a year — and Lisa will tell you that, too, because the method only works if the advice is honest.
