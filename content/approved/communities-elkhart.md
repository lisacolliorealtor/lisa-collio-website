# ARTICLE 1: What Is Elkhart, Indiana Known For?

Elkhart is the largest city in Elkhart County, and its identity is closely tied to a single industry: recreational vehicle manufacturing.

Elkhart County — with Elkhart at its center — is commonly referred to as the RV Capital of the World, producing the large majority of recreational vehicles built in the United States. Several major manufacturers are headquartered directly in Elkhart, including Thor Industries (which owns brands like Airstream, Jayco, and Heartland) and Forest River. Lippert Components, a major RV parts supplier, also has significant operations in the Elkhart/Middlebury area.

This concentration of manufacturing shapes much of the local economy — a large share of jobs in the area connect, directly or indirectly, to RV production and its supply chain.

Beyond manufacturing, Elkhart is also home to cultural landmarks including the historic Lerner Theatre and Wellfield Botanic Gardens, giving residents access to arts and recreation alongside the city's industrial identity.

For anyone relocating for work in the RV industry, or simply curious what defines the city, this manufacturing base is the single most important fact to understand about Elkhart.

# ARTICLE 2: What School District Serves Elkhart, Indiana?

Elkhart is served by Elkhart Community Schools, one of the larger school districts in the region.

The district includes 13 elementary schools, 3 middle schools, and Elkhart High School — which operates as a two-campus system, with a Main Campus and a separate Freshman Division. The district also includes alternative and career-center campuses. Total enrollment runs above 11,000 students.

Elkhart Community Schools serves not only the city of Elkhart itself but also the nearby communities of Bristol and Simonton Lake.

As with any school search, the right fit depends on your own family's needs and priorities. The district's own resources are the best starting point for current enrollment boundaries and program details — this article is meant to help you know what exists, not to recommend one school over another.

# ARTICLE 3: What Industries and Employers Are in Elkhart, Indiana?

Elkhart's economy is defined by one industry more than any other: recreational vehicle (RV) manufacturing.

Elkhart County as a whole is often called the RV Capital of the World, and Elkhart itself is home to some of the largest names in the industry. Thor Industries — one of the largest RV manufacturers in the world, and owner of brands including Airstream, Jayco, and Heartland — is headquartered in Elkhart. Forest River, another major manufacturer, is also based in the city. Lippert Components, a significant supplier to the RV industry, operates extensively in the Elkhart and nearby Middlebury area.

Together, this concentration of manufacturers and suppliers means the large majority of RVs sold in the United States trace back to this part of Indiana — and a substantial share of local employment connects to this industry, whether directly on a manufacturing floor or through the broader supply chain.

For anyone considering a move to Elkhart for RV-industry work, or simply wanting to understand what drives the local economy, this manufacturing base is central to daily life in the city.

# ARTICLE 4: What Landmarks and Attractions Does Elkhart, Indiana Have?

Elkhart offers a mix of cultural and recreational landmarks that reflect both its history and its identity as a manufacturing hub.

The Lerner Theatre is one of the city's most recognized landmarks — a historic performance venue in downtown Elkhart that continues to host concerts and events. Wellfield Botanic Gardens offers a different kind of destination, with cultivated gardens and green space for residents and visitors.

Reflecting the city's manufacturing identity directly, the RV/MH Hall of Fame & Museum sits on a roughly 40-acre campus in Elkhart and draws more than 15,000 visitors annually. Beyond its museum exhibits, the campus also hosts industry events tied to the region's RV manufacturing business.

Together, these landmarks give Elkhart a combination of historic arts and culture, green space, and a direct connection to the industry that shapes the region.

# ARTICLE 5: Where Is Elkhart, Indiana Located Relative to Nearby Cities?

For anyone relocating to the area, understanding Elkhart's location relative to nearby cities is a practical part of planning a move.

Elkhart sits in the northern part of Indiana, just east of South Bend and Mishawaka — meaning residents have relatively easy access to the amenities and airport of the greater South Bend area while living in a smaller city. Within Elkhart County itself, Elkhart is located about 25 minutes northeast of Goshen, the county seat, making it easy to move between the two cities for work, shopping, or visiting family.

This geography is one reason many buyers compare Elkhart and Goshen directly when deciding where to settle — both cities sit within a short drive of each other, while each offers its own distinct character and housing options.
