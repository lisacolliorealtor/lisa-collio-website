# ARTICLE 1: What Is Goshen, Indiana Known For?

Goshen is a small Indiana city with a distinct identity, shaped by a few things that come up again and again when people describe it.

The most visible is downtown Goshen — a closely clustered business district with a density that's unusual for a city its size. Independent shops, restaurants, and monthly community events like First Fridays give downtown an active, small-town feel rather than the strip-mall pattern common in many similar-sized cities.

Goshen is also home to Goshen College, a small liberal arts college known for its international education programs. The college's presence adds a level of cultural exchange and community programming that's uncommon in a city this size.

Economically, Goshen sits within one of the most concentrated manufacturing regions in the country: Elkhart County is often called the RV Capital of the World, with the majority of U.S. recreational vehicle production happening in the Elkhart–Middlebury–Goshen area. Many residents work directly or indirectly in this industry.

For anyone considering Goshen as a place to live, these three things — a compact, closely clustered downtown, an international college presence, and a strong regional manufacturing economy — are the throughlines that shape daily life in the city.

# ARTICLE 2: What School District Serves Goshen, Indiana?

Goshen is served by Goshen Community Schools, the public school district covering the city.

The district includes nine schools: Goshen High School (home of the RedHawks), Goshen Junior High School, Goshen Intermediate School, Chandler Innovation Academy, and five elementary schools. Total enrollment runs in the range of 6,200–6,500 students depending on the school year.

Goshen Community Schools is known for its diversity and international character, which reflects the broader community — including the presence of Goshen College, which brings students and families from around the world to the area.

For families researching schools as part of a move, the district's website (goshenschools.org) is the best source for current enrollment boundaries, program details, and school-specific information. Every family's priorities are different, and the right fit depends on your own children's needs — this article is meant as a starting point for your own research, not a recommendation of one school over another.

# ARTICLE 3: What Is Downtown Goshen Like?

Downtown Goshen stands out for how closely its businesses are clustered together — a density that's rarer than it might seem for a city of its size in northern Indiana.

The downtown district is home to a strong base of independent, locally-owned businesses: shops, restaurants, and cafes that give the area a distinct character rather than a generic commercial strip. One of the most visited spots is the Old Bag Factory, a historic industrial building that's been converted into a collection of shops and artist studios — a good example of how Goshen has preserved its historic architecture while giving it new life.

Downtown also hosts regular community events, including First Fridays, a monthly gathering that brings together local businesses, art, and residents.

For anyone interested in a downtown where dinner, shopping, and community events sit close together rather than spread across a wider area, downtown Goshen's layout and business mix are worth knowing about as part of understanding what day-to-day life in the city looks like.

# ARTICLE 4: What Industries and Employers Are in the Goshen Area?

Understanding the local economy is a useful part of researching any place you might move to — and in Goshen's case, the economic story is closely tied to the broader Elkhart County region.

Elkhart County is home to the majority of U.S. recreational vehicle (RV) manufacturing — commonly cited as more than 80% of global RV production concentrated in the Elkhart–Middlebury–Goshen area. Major manufacturers with operations in the region include Thor Industries (headquartered in Elkhart and owner of brands like Airstream and Jayco), Forest River, Keystone RV, Lippert Components, and others. This concentration of manufacturing means a significant share of local jobs — directly or through supporting industries — connect back to RV production.

Within Goshen itself, two employers stand out locally: Goshen College, the small liberal arts college that anchors much of the city's cultural and educational life, and IU Health Goshen Hospital, the area's primary hospital and a major local employer.

Downtown Goshen also supports a healthy base of small, independently owned businesses — part of what gives the compact downtown district its character.

For anyone relocating for a job in the RV industry specifically, Goshen's location within this manufacturing region is one of the most practical facts to know about the area.

# ARTICLE 5: What Landmarks and Amenities Does Goshen Have?

Goshen offers a mix of local landmarks and easy access to attractions throughout Elkhart County.

Within the city, the Old Bag Factory is one of the most recognized landmarks — a converted historic industrial building now home to shops, studios, and small businesses. Downtown Goshen itself functions as a kind of ongoing amenity, with its closely clustered layout and regular events like First Fridays bringing the community together on a recurring basis.

Beyond the city limits, Goshen residents have convenient access to attractions throughout the wider county, including Wellfield Botanic Gardens and the historic Lerner Theatre, both in nearby Elkhart, and the RV/MH Hall of Fame & Museum, which draws more than 15,000 visitors annually and also hosts industry events on its roughly 40-acre campus.

Together, these local and regional landmarks give Goshen residents a combination of small-town amenities close to home and easy access to larger attractions nearby.
