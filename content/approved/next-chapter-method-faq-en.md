## ARTICLE 1: What Is The Next Chapter Method™?

The Next Chapter Method™ is Lisa Collio's structured approach to guiding clients through buying or selling a home — six steps, always in the same order: Discover, Educate, Unlock, Execute, Transition, and Celebrate.

What makes it different from simply "listing a house" or "showing properties" is where it starts. Instead of opening with square footage and price ranges, the Method opens with a conversation about you — what matters most, what you're protecting, what's driving the timing of this move. That understanding shapes everything that follows: how options get explained, what strategy gets built, and how the transaction itself gets handled.

The framework also recognizes that a real estate transaction doesn't end at closing. The Transition step deliberately extends support into life after the paperwork is signed — connecting clients with trusted local professionals and community resources as they settle into whatever comes next, whether that's a new neighborhood, a smaller home, or a fresh start after a major life change.

The Method exists because Lisa noticed a pattern over years of working with families in Goshen and Elkhart: the clients who felt most confident weren't the ones who got the "perfect" house — they were the ones who understood *why* they were making the choices they made. The Next Chapter Method™ is built to create that understanding every time.

## ARTICLE 2: How Is The Next Chapter Method™ Different From a Typical Buying or Selling Process?

Most real estate transactions follow a property-first sequence: find a listing, tour it, make an offer, negotiate, close. It works, but it treats every client's situation as essentially the same process with different addresses.

The Next Chapter Method™ inverts that starting point. Before any property enters the conversation, the Discover step focuses entirely on the person: What matters most to you right now? What are you trying to protect — stability for your kids, proximity to aging parents, a specific financial goal? What's actually driving the timing of this move? These aren't small-talk questions — the answers directly shape the strategy that follows.

This matters because the "right" choice in real estate is rarely just about the property itself. A family relocating for a new job has different priorities than someone downsizing after their children have moved out, even if they're looking at homes in the same price range. A typical process treats both clients identically. The Next Chapter Method™ treats them as the different situations they actually are.

The six-step structure also means nothing gets skipped in the rush to close a deal. Education happens before pressure. Strategy gets built before execution begins. And support continues after the transaction, through the Transition step, rather than ending the moment keys change hands.

## ARTICLE 3: Does Using The Next Chapter Method™ Cost Anything Extra?

No — there's no additional fee or special package involved. The Next Chapter Method™ is simply how Lisa Collio approaches every client relationship, built into her standard service at the same commission structure any client would expect when working with a licensed real estate agent.

Think of it less as an add-on product and more as a way of organizing the work Lisa would be doing for you regardless — understanding your situation, educating you on your options, building a pricing or search strategy, handling negotiations and paperwork, and supporting you through the transition. The Method simply gives that work a consistent, intentional structure instead of leaving it to chance or to whatever a given transaction happens to require in the moment.

If you're comparing agents and wondering whether "the Method" is a premium service tier, it isn't — it's Lisa's baseline standard for how she works with every buyer and seller in Goshen and Elkhart, whether it's someone's first home purchase or a fifth investment property.

## ARTICLE 4: How Long Does Each Step of The Next Chapter Method™ Take?

There's no fixed timeline attached to the six steps — and that's intentional. The Method describes an *order*, not a schedule, because every client's situation moves at a different pace.

For some clients, Discover might happen in a single conversation — the priorities are clear, the timing is set, and the next step follows quickly. For others, especially during a major life transition like a divorce, a loss, or an unexpected relocation, that first step might take longer, and that's completely appropriate. Rushing understanding to hit an arbitrary schedule defeats the purpose of the step.

Similarly, Execute — the step covering pricing, marketing, negotiation, and paperwork — can unfold over a few weeks for a straightforward sale in a fast-moving market, or longer if a property needs more preparation or the search takes time to find the right fit.

What stays consistent is the order and the intention behind each step, not the calendar. Lisa's role is to move at the pace that's right for your situation, while making sure no step gets skipped just to move faster.

## ARTICLE 5: What If I'm Not Sure Which Life Chapter I'm In?

This is one of the most common feelings clients bring to that first conversation — and it's not a problem to solve before you talk to Lisa. It's exactly what the Discover step exists for.

The five Life Chapters — Starting Out, Growing, Changing Direction, Simplifying, and Legacy — are patterns Lisa has noticed over years of working with families in Goshen and Elkhart, not boxes you're expected to sort yourself into beforehand. Many people arrive somewhere in between, or with a situation that blends elements of more than one chapter — someone simplifying their home while also protecting a legacy for their kids, for example.

The point of naming these patterns isn't to label you — it's to give Lisa language for asking better questions. Instead of a generic "what are you looking for," the Life Chapters framework helps surface the real question underneath: What am I becoming? How do I move forward after things changed? How do I take care of the people who matter most?

If you're not sure where you fit, that uncertainty itself is useful information — and working through it together is the first real step of the Method, not a prerequisite for starting it.
