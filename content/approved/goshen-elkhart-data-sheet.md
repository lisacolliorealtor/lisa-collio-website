# Goshen & Elkhart, Indiana — Reference Data Sheet

**Purpose:** A sourced, internal reference for Claude (and anyone drafting copy) to pull accurate community facts from — population, economy, schools, geography — when writing website pages, blog articles, or community guides. This is a research tool, not publishable copy; language still needs to be adapted per brand voice and compliance rules before it goes on the site.

**Compiled:** July 16, 2026
**Recommended refresh cadence:** Annually (this data moves slowly — population/income update yearly with new Census releases; employer/school facts rarely change year to year). Re-check before any major site refresh or if a figure is being used in a specific claim.

---

## ⚠️ Fair Housing caution — read before using any of this

Some data below (ethnicity, religion, national origin) is included **for background understanding only** — to help write copy that's accurate and respectful, not to describe "who lives here" or "who a neighborhood is for." Per the Fair Housing compliance reference already in this project:

- Never use demographic composition to describe a neighborhood as suited to a particular group.
- Never frame diversity, religious presence (e.g., Amish community), or income level as a buyer-fit signal ("great for young families," "quiet retiree community," etc.).
- Population, schools, and employer facts are fine to use factually (e.g., "Goshen is home to Goshen College" or "the median commute is about 19 minutes"). Anything that implies who "belongs" in an area is off-limits.

## 3. Major Employers

**Elkhart — "RV Capital of the World"**
- Elkhart County is home to the majority of the U.S. RV manufacturing industry — commonly cited as 80%+ of global RV production concentrated in the Elkhart–Middlebury–Goshen area.
- Major manufacturers headquartered or with major operations here: **Thor Industries** (HQ Elkhart; owns Airstream, Jayco, Heartland, and other brands), **Forest River** (Elkhart), **Keystone RV**, **Lippert Components** (major supplier, Elkhart/Middlebury), **Jayco** (Middlebury), **Winnebago**, **Gulf Stream Coach**.
- This is the single most useful economic fact for buyer-relocation content — many buyers are moving here *for* RV-industry jobs.

**Goshen**
- **Goshen College** — small liberal arts college, known for international education and study-abroad programs; adds to local diversity and offers community-facing courses.
- **IU Health Goshen Hospital** — the area's primary hospital and a major employer.
- **Goshen Community Schools** — public school district, also a significant local employer (see below).
- Downtown Goshen has a notable base of small/independent businesses (relevant to the "walkable downtown" lifestyle angle already used in your articles).

*Sources: Lippert company blog; RV PRO industry trade coverage; Wikipedia (Thor Industries); Goshen Community Schools "About Goshen" page.*

## 4. School Districts

**Goshen Community Schools**
- 9 schools, ~6,200–6,500 students depending on source/year
- Includes Goshen High School (home of the RedHawks), Goshen Junior High, Goshen Intermediate, Chandler Innovation Academy, and 5 elementary schools
- District description emphasizes diversity and international character (consistent with Goshen College's presence)

**Elkhart Community Schools**
- 13 elementary, 3 middle, 1 high school (plus alternative and career-center campuses), ~11,000+ students
- Serves most of Elkhart plus Bristol and Simonton Lake
- Elkhart High School operates as a two-campus system (Main Campus / Freshman Division)

**Use in copy:** Naming the district and a school by name ("Goshen High School," "Elkhart Community Schools") is factual and safe. Avoid ranking schools as "good" or "bad" or tying school quality to neighborhood recommendations in a way that could read as steering — describe programs/facts, not implied quality judgments tied to who lives there.

*Sources: Goshen Community Schools website; Wikipedia (Goshen High School, Elkhart Community Schools); U.S. News Education; NCES district search.*

## 5. Landmarks & Community Character (already reflected in your existing articles)

- **Downtown Goshen** — walkable, active small-business district; home to **First Fridays** (monthly downtown event)
- **Old Bag Factory** — historic building turned shops/studios, Goshen
- **Wellfield Botanic Gardens** — Elkhart
- **Lerner Theatre** — historic downtown Elkhart performance venue
- **RV/MH Hall of Fame & Museum** — Elkhart, draws 15,000+ visitors annually, also hosts business/industry events at its ~40-acre campus
- Elkhart and LaGrange counties together form the third-largest Amish settlement in the U.S. — a real regional fact, but per the fair housing caution above, this should only ever appear as neutral background (e.g., regional tourism/context), never as a buyer-fit or neighborhood description.

*Sources: RV/MH Hall of Fame (via Indeed employer listing); Wikipedia (Amish in Indiana) — regional context only, use with care per compliance note above.*
