## What the data shows

- Homes across Elkhart and Goshen are selling in under two weeks on average, at or above 98% of asking price.
- South Elkhart (46516) has the highest sell-through rate in the county at 87.9% — nearly 9 in 10 homes that list, sell.

- East Goshen (46528) has the fastest median time to sale at just 6 days; West Elkhart (46517) follows closely at 7 days.
- Not every listing sells — 118 listings expired county-wide without selling, a reminder that pricing strategy matters even in a fast market.
- The most active price band countywide is $200,000–$350,000, where 507 of the year's 677 sales happened.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often is this page updated?** Quarterly, using data pulled directly from the Elkhart County Board of REALTORS® MLS. The date range above always reflects the most recent update.

**Is this data specific to Goshen and Elkhart, or all of Elkhart County?** Both — the county-wide figures cover all of Elkhart County, while the neighborhood breakdown focuses on specific ZIP codes within Elkhart and Goshen.

**Why do active listing prices look higher than recent sale prices?** Active listings reflect what's currently on the market, which can run ahead of what's actually closing — a gap worth watching if you're pricing a home to sell or budgeting a purchase.

**Does a fast market mean I'll get more than asking price?** Not necessarily — a high list-to-sale ratio (in the high 90s%) generally means homes are selling close to asking, not dramatically over it. Bidding wars happen but aren't the norm reflected in these countywide numbers.

**Where does this data come from?** The Elkhart County Board of REALTORS® MLS (ECBOR) — the same source licensed agents use, not a public estimate site like Zillow or Redfin.
