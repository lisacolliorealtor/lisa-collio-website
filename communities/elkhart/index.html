About Elkhart

Elkhart is home to roughly 54,000 residents, making it the largest city in Elkhart County, in the northern part of Indiana. The city sits just east of South Bend and Mishawaka, about 25 minutes northwest of Goshen — the county seat. Elkhart County as a whole counts roughly 208,000 residents across 463 square miles and was founded in 1830.

Around Town

Elkhart's civic landmarks include the Wellfield Botanic Gardens, the Lerner Theatre — a historic downtown performance venue — and the RV/MH Hall of Fame & Museum, which draws more than 15,000 visitors annually and hosts business and industry events on its roughly 40-acre campus.

Working Here

Elkhart is known as the "RV Capital of the World." Elkhart County is home to the majority of the U.S. RV manufacturing industry — the Elkhart–Middlebury–Goshen area is commonly cited as accounting for more than 80% of global RV production. Major manufacturers headquartered or with major operations here include Thor Industries (headquartered in Elkhart; owner of Airstream, Jayco, Heartland, and other brands), Forest River, Keystone RV, Lippert Components, Jayco, Winnebago, and Gulf Stream Coach.

Schools

Elkhart Community Schools is the city's public school district, with 13 elementary schools, 3 middle schools, and 1 high school, plus alternative and career-center campuses, serving more than 11,000 students. The district serves most of Elkhart plus Bristol and Simonton Lake, and Elkhart High School operates as a two-campus system (Main Campus / Freshman Division).

Getting Around

Elkhart sits in Elkhart County, Indiana, just east of South Bend and Mishawaka. Goshen, the county seat, is about 25 minutes to the southeast.
