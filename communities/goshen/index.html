Living in Goshen, Indiana

Goshen is where Lisa lives, works, and has helped dozens of families put down roots. This page is a plain-facts introduction to the city — what's here, what it's known for, and what to expect — for everyone thinking about calling Goshen home.

About Goshen

Goshen is home to roughly 34,000 residents and serves as the county seat of Elkhart County, in the northern part of Indiana. The city sits about 25 minutes southeast of Elkhart, and both cities are part of Elkhart County — a county of roughly 208,000 residents covering 463 square miles, founded in 1830.

Around Town

Downtown Goshen is a walkable, active small-business district, and it hosts First Fridays, a monthly downtown event. The Old Bag Factory — a historic building turned shops and studios — is another Goshen landmark, and the downtown area is known for its notable base of small and independent businesses.

Working Here

Goshen College, a small liberal arts college known for international education and study-abroad programs, offers community-facing courses. IU Health Goshen Hospital is the area's primary hospital and a major employer, and Goshen Community Schools — the public school district — is also a significant local employer. Regionally, the Elkhart–Middlebury–Goshen area is commonly cited as home to more than 80% of global RV production.

Schools

Goshen Community Schools is the city's public school district, with 9 schools serving roughly 6,200 to 6,500 students. The district includes Goshen High School (home of the RedHawks), Goshen Junior High, Goshen Intermediate, Chandler Innovation Academy, and five elementary schools.

Getting Around

Goshen sits in Elkhart County, Indiana, about 25 minutes southeast of the city of Elkhart. Elkhart itself lies just east of South Bend and Mishawaka.
