Why Should I Sell My Home in Goshen, Indiana with Lisa Collio?

Selling a Goshen home usually means selling more than a property — for many owners it’s the house where the kids grew up, in a town they know street by street. A decision that personal deserves better than a generic listing process run from somewhere else. It deserves a plan, built by someone who knows this market because she works and lives in it.

The short answer: selling your home in Goshen, Indiana with Lisa Collio means working with an agent based right here in Goshen — with a clear 12-step process, pricing built from local data, professional marketing to two buyer pools, and communication that arrives before you have to ask for it.

Based in Goshen, not just serving it

Lisa’s office is on Elkhart Road in Goshen, and the market knowledge behind her pricing and positioning is local in the literal sense — which neighborhoods are drawing buyers right now, how homes near downtown price against homes on the edge of town, what Goshen buyers walk in looking for. In a market this size, that block-level reading is frequently the difference between a price that works and one that almost does.

A 12-step process instead of a leap

For owners who haven’t sold in a decade or two, the process itself is half the stress — what happens when, what decisions are coming, what could go wrong. Lisa’s 12-step selling process turns that fog into a sequence: valuation, preparation, pricing strategy, launch, showings, offers, negotiation, and the path to closing, each explained before you’re in the middle of it. You always know where you are and what comes next, which is most of what “a smooth sale” actually means.

Priced from data, the first time

Overpricing is the number one reason homes sit, and it usually enters through flattery — an agent quoting a hopeful number to win the listing. Lisa’s pricing conversation is built the opposite way: recent comparable Goshen sales, the current competition, and an honest read of your home’s condition, assembled into a price designed to create demand in the critical first two weeks. Truth over pressure, strategy over hype — even when the truthful number is the harder conversation.

And honest pricing doesn’t mean leaving money behind — it means fighting for the real number. As Cristina L. put it in her Google review: “She will go beyond limits to get the best deal for you.”

Marketing and reporting at full strength

Every Goshen listing launches with professional photography, video, drone footage, 360° tours, and floor plans, distributed everywhere buyers search and marketed to both English- and Spanish-speaking buyer pools — a wider audience than most listings ever reach. Then, twice a week, you see exactly how it’s performing: viewings, showings, feedback, and what the data suggests, so every decision gets made on numbers rather than nerves.

A story about the process

A Goshen couple who’d been in their home for nineteen years almost didn’t list at all — not because they weren’t ready to move, but because the process felt like a cliff. They’d bought the house in a different era of real estate and had no idea what selling even looked like now. What changed their mind wasn’t a pitch; it was a plan. Lisa walked them through all twelve steps in the first meeting — what would happen, in what order, and which decisions were actually theirs to make. The staging consult told them exactly what to do (and, mercifully, what to skip). The reports came twice a week. The sale closed without a single moment of “what’s happening now?” — which, they said afterward, was the part they’d feared most and the part that never came.

Credentials you can check, terms you can trust

The record is verifiable: 120+ homes sold, $20M+ in closed sales, top 20% of Elkhart County agents, with RE/MAX Results as a member of The Viruez Team. As a Seniors Real Estate Specialist (SRES®), Lisa is additionally trained for long-time owners navigating downsizing or family transitions. And the listing agreement carries a cancel-anytime guarantee — confidence in the service, written into the terms.
