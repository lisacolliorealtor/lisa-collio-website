What Is a Seniors Real Estate Specialist (SRES®) and How Can One Help in Goshen and Elkhart, Indiana?

If you or a family member are weighing a move in Goshen or Elkhart — downsizing, transitioning toward retirement living, or selling a home the family has owned for decades — you may have come across the letters SRES® next to an agent’s name and wondered whether they mean anything in practice. They do, and the situations where they matter are exactly the ones where a standard transaction process falls short.

The short answer: a Seniors Real Estate Specialist (SRES®) is a REALTOR® with specialized training in helping clients aged 50+ and their families navigate housing decisions tied to major life transitions — downsizing, relocating, retirement moves, and selling long-time family homes. Lisa Collio holds the SRES® designation and brings that training to families in Goshen and Elkhart, Indiana.

What the designation actually means

The SRES® designation reflects additional training focused on the realities of later-life moves: the financial questions around equity and what comes next, the emotional weight of leaving a home full of years, and the family dynamics that come with decisions involving adult children and caregivers. It isn’t a different kind of real estate — it’s the same transaction handled by someone trained for everything surrounding it.

Why it matters in Goshen and Elkhart specifically

Many homeowners here have been in their homes for decades, which usually means two things at once: significant equity, and a house that’s accumulated a lot of life. The decisions that follow — sell or stay, what to update, how to prepare a home holding twenty-five years of belongings, how to coordinate a family spread across time zones — are bigger than a listing agreement. Having someone who has guided that exact situation before makes each step feel less like a leap.

When working with an SRES® makes sense

Not everyone needs one, but a few situations reliably benefit. Downsizing is the most common: the house feels too large, maintenance has become a burden, and simpler living sounds better every winter — an SRES® helps evaluate options, right-size the next home, and plan the transition in real steps. Helping a parent sell comes next, and it’s often the most delicate; adult children stepping in to assist with a long-time home, a move closer to family, or a transition to assisted living need someone who can guide the conversations as carefully as the paperwork. And moves into retirement communities or 55+ housing involve coordination — timing the sale, preparing the home, understanding costs — that rewards a planned approach over an improvised one.

What makes these moves different

Real estate decisions later in life are rarely just financial. Selling a long-time home means leaving memories and changing routines, and that deserves patience rather than a countdown clock. The financial side has its own weight — how much equity is really there, what the next living situation costs, what makes sense over a ten-year horizon rather than a ten-week one. And most of these decisions involve family: children, caregivers, sometimes several opinions at once. Clear communication isn’t a nice-to-have in that environment; it’s the whole job.

How Lisa approaches it

The process starts with a clear plan — defined steps, honest timelines, and one decision at a time instead of everything at once. From there, Lisa connects families with local resources as needed, from downsizing and decluttering help to estate sale companies and moving assistance in the Goshen and Elkhart area, and keeps everyone informed throughout: what’s happening, what’s next, and which decisions actually need to be made now versus later. The goal is a transition that feels managed, not endured.

A story about downsizing

A homeowner in Elkhart had been in the same house for more than twenty-five years and knew it was time to downsize — but couldn’t find the starting line. Where to begin, what the home needed, what the options even were: all of it blurred together into a reason to wait another year. Working with Lisa, it became a sequence instead of a wall. They identified the handful of updates that genuinely mattered, brought in local help for the decluttering, and priced the home strategically. The sale went smoothly, and the move landed them in a home that actually fits their life now. Looking back, the turning point wasn’t any single decision — it was having a step-by-step plan where an overwhelming whole used to be.

Mistakes worth avoiding

The patterns Lisa sees most often: waiting too long to start planning, which shrinks the options; trying to do everything at once instead of in steps; not exploring the full range of housing choices, which is usually wider than families assume; and rushing decisions that deserve a little air. None of these are failures — they’re just what happens without a plan, and all of them are avoidable with one.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does SRES® stand for?

Seniors Real Estate Specialist — a designation for REALTORS® who have completed specialized training in serving clients 50+ and their families through housing transitions.

Does working with an SRES® cost more?

No — the designation doesn’t add cost. It adds training and experience for situations where a standard process isn’t enough.

Can an SRES® help families, not just seniors?

Yes. Many of Lisa’s SRES® clients are adult children helping a parent through a sale or transition — the designation covers exactly that situation.

Final thoughts

Housing decisions later in life are about more than property — they’re about your lifestyle, your future, and your family, all at once. The right guidance doesn’t remove the weight of those decisions, but it makes them navigable, one clear step at a time.

If you or someone in your family is considering a transition in Goshen or Elkhart and wants a plan instead of a pile of questions, the best next step is a conversation.
