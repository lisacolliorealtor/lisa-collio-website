Elkhart County Mid-Year Market Update (2026)

Halfway through 2026, the numbers tell a clear story: homes across Goshen and Elkhart are selling quickly, close to full asking price, and demand hasn't let up. Here's what the data actually shows — and what it means whether you're thinking about listing or buying.

The county at a glance

From January 1 through July 16, 2026, 677 homes sold across Elkhart County. The median sale price landed at $255,000, with an average of $277,456. Homes are moving fast: the median time on market was just 9 days, and sellers are getting nearly full value, with a list-to-sale price ratio of 98.62%.

Not every listed home sells, though. The sell-through rate — the share of listings that actually close — sits at 71.72%. Overpriced homes are the clearest exception to the "sells fast" story: 118 listings expired county-wide without selling. Pricing right from the start still matters more than almost anything else in this market.

As of July 16, 2026, there were 250 active listings, with a median list price of $345,000 — notably higher than the year's median sale price, a gap worth watching if you're pricing a home or budgeting a purchase.

Market conditions vary block by block. Here's how five ZIP codes across Elkhart and Goshen compared over the same period.

A few things stand out:

  • South Elkhart (46516) has the highest sell-through rate in the county — 87.9% of listings there sold, meaning nearly 9 in 10 homes that list, sell.
  • East Goshen (46528) has the fastest median time to sale in the group, at just 6 days, with West Elkhart (46517) close behind at 7 days.
  • Downtown Goshen (46526) sellers are getting the closest to full price, averaging 99.37% of list price.
  • The most active price band countywide is $200,000–$350,000, where 507 of the year's 677 sales happened — this is where competition is sharpest.

What this means if you're selling

A 9-day median time on market and a 98%+ list-to-sale ratio both point the same direction: well-priced homes in good condition are moving quickly, often at or close to asking. That's a strong environment to sell in — but the 118 expired listings are a reminder that "the market is hot" doesn't rescue an overpriced home. Pricing strategy still decides the outcome more than timing does.

What this means if you're buying

Fast sales and high list-to-sale ratios mean less room to negotiate on the right home, especially in the $200,000–$350,000 range where most of the year's activity is concentrated. Getting pre-approved and ready to move before you find the home — not after — makes the difference in this kind of market.
