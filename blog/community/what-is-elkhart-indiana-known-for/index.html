What Is Elkhart, Indiana Known For?

Elkhart is the largest city in Elkhart County, and its identity is closely tied to a single industry: recreational vehicle manufacturing.

Elkhart County — with Elkhart at its center — is commonly referred to as the RV Capital of the World, producing the large majority of recreational vehicles built in the United States. Several major manufacturers are headquartered directly in Elkhart, including Thor Industries (which owns brands like Airstream, Jayco, and Heartland) and Forest River. Lippert Components, a major RV parts supplier, also has significant operations in the Elkhart/Middlebury area.

This concentration of manufacturing shapes much of the local economy — a large share of jobs in the area connect, directly or indirectly, to RV production and its supply chain.

Beyond manufacturing, Elkhart is also home to cultural landmarks including the historic Lerner Theatre and Wellfield Botanic Gardens, giving residents access to arts and recreation alongside the city's industrial identity.

For anyone relocating for work in the RV industry, or simply curious what defines the city, this manufacturing base is the single most important fact to understand about Elkhart.
