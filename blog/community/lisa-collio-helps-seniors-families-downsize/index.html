How Does Lisa Collio Help Seniors and Families Downsize in Goshen and Elkhart, Indiana?

Downsizing is rarely about square footage. It's about leaving a house that held twenty-five years of a family's life, usually while coordinating opinions from three generations, and making financial decisions that shape the next decade. A standard transaction process isn't built for that — which is exactly why the SRES® designation exists, and why Lisa earned it.

The short answer: as a Seniors Real Estate Specialist (SRES®), Lisa Collio helps seniors and their families in Goshen and Elkhart turn an overwhelming transition into a sequence — a clear plan, one decision at a time, with local resources connected and every family member kept informed from first conversation to closing.

It starts with a plan, not a listing agreement

The first meeting isn't about the sale. It's about the situation: what's driving the move, what the timeline honestly allows, what the equity in the home makes possible, and who in the family needs to be part of which decisions. From there the transition becomes a sequence — evaluate options, prepare the home, price it strategically, coordinate the move — with each step explained before it arrives. Overwhelm usually isn't about the size of the task; it's about facing it as one undifferentiated wall. The plan is what turns the wall into stairs.

Preparing a home that holds decades

A long-time home has accumulated a lot of life, and preparing it to sell is often the step families dread most. Lisa keeps it disciplined: identify the handful of updates that genuinely matter (and skip the rest), bring in local help where it's needed — decluttering services, estate sale companies, moving assistance in the Goshen and Elkhart area — and schedule it all backward from the target listing date. The goal is a home ready to sell at full strength without asking anyone to relive every box.

Working with the whole family

Many of these transitions involve adult children helping a parent — sometimes from across the country — and clear communication stops being a courtesy and becomes the whole job. Lisa keeps everyone who should be informed, informed: what's happening, what's next, and which decisions actually need to be made now versus later. One real review hints at how she works with families: Natalie T. wrote, "I'm very grateful that Lisa helped my parents with getting a dream house that we've been looking for. I also liked getting to see how kind and generous she is while speaking to her." That was a purchase rather than a downsizing sale — but the skill it describes, earning both the parents' and the adult child's confidence at once, is precisely the one these transitions demand.

The emotional side gets treated as part of the job

Selling a long-time home means leaving memories, and that deserves patience rather than a countdown clock. Lisa's process is deliberately paced for it — decisions get air, nothing is rushed to closing that shouldn't be, and the moments that carry weight are treated as part of the transaction, not an obstacle to it.

A story about finding the starting line

One Elkhart homeowner had been in the same house for more than twenty-five years and knew it was time to downsize — but couldn't find where to begin. What the home needed, what the options were, what order any of it went in: it all blurred into a reason to wait another year. Working with Lisa, it became a sequence instead of a wall. A short list of updates that actually mattered, local help for the decluttering, a strategic price, and a sale that led to a home that fits this chapter of life. Looking back, the turning point wasn't any single decision — it was having a step-by-step plan where an overwhelming whole used to be.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the SRES® designation mean?

Seniors Real Estate Specialist — a REALTOR® with specialized training in helping clients 50+ and their families through housing transitions. The full explainer is in its own article on this site.

Can Lisa help if I'm assisting my parents with a sale?

Yes — adult children helping a parent through a sale or transition is one of the most common SRES® situations, and family coordination is built into the process.

Does working with an SRES® cost more?

No. The designation adds training and experience, not cost — and the initial consultation is free with no obligation.

Final thoughts

Downsizing decisions are about lifestyle, family, and the next decade all at once. The right guidance doesn't shrink the decision — it makes it navigable, one clear step at a time.

If you or someone in your family is weighing a transition in Goshen or Elkhart, the best next step is a conversation.
