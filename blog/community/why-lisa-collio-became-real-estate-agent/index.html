Why Did Lisa Collio Become a Real Estate Agent in Goshen and Elkhart, Indiana?

Most agent bios start with a license year and a sales number. Lisa's story starts a few thousand miles from Indiana, in homes that were never quite hers — and that's not a detail. It's the reason she does this work the way she does it.

The short answer: Lisa Collio became a real estate agent because she knows, firsthand, what it means to live without a permanent home — and what it means to finally have one. Raised in Latin America as the daughter of missionaries, she grew up in temporary houses in other people's countries. Helping families in Goshen and Elkhart find lasting stability became the mission behind the career: transforming dreams into homes.

A childhood of temporary homes

Growing up as a missionary kid meant moving between places that never fully belonged to her family — homes borrowed, rented, passed through. That kind of childhood teaches things a settled one doesn't: adaptability, service, how to build community from scratch. It also leaves a permanent understanding of what a stable home is worth, precisely because it was never guaranteed.

The moment it became a mission

Years later, Lisa watched people who had given everything to serving others struggle to secure stable housing as they got older. That landed hard, and it stayed. Homeownership stopped looking like a transaction category and started looking like what it actually is for most families: the foundation under everything else — stability, equity, a place the next chapter can be built on. Real estate became the way to put that conviction to work.

The teaching heart behind the education-first approach

Lisa's background runs through Bethel College, the Word of Life Bible Institute, and the Children's Ministries Institute® — and she still teaches junior high students at Refuge Church, where she serves alongside her husband and leads the ladies' ministry. That teaching instinct is all over how she practices real estate: explain each step before the client is in the middle of it, never assume prior knowledge, and treat questions as the point of the process rather than an interruption to it. Clarity over confusion isn't a slogan on her wall; it's how a teacher works.

Family at the center

Lisa and her husband, Francisco, have been married more than twenty-three years and are parents to Amy and Nathan. Family is the center of her life — and it's also the lens she brings to client work, because nearly every transaction she handles is really a family decision wearing a contract: the first home, the bigger one, the smaller one after the kids leave, the one closer to the grandkids.

In her own words

When a client once thanked her for bilingual service in a Google review, Lisa's public reply said more about her reasons than any bio paragraph could: "I feel honored to be able to help families achieve the American dream of owning their own home and to provide them with assistance in their own language." That's the mission, stated to a real client, in the middle of real work — not written for a website.

Why Goshen and Elkhart

For someone whose childhood had no permanent hometown, Goshen is a chosen one — home, church, office on Elkhart Road, and the community her family serves. Working exclusively in Goshen and Elkhart is part of the same choice: depth over territory, neighbors over leads.
