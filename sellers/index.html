Key Steps to Selling Your Home

Price It Right From the Start

Here's the thing about overpricing — it feels safe until it isn't. You figure you can always come down later, but by the time you do, the damage is already done. Buyers have seen it sitting. They start assuming something's off. Lisa looks at what's actually sold nearby, what's been sitting and why, and what buyers are willing to pay right now — not six months ago. That's the number you want to start with.

Get Your Home Ready Without Going Overboard

Most sellers either do too much or not enough. Lisa's pretty good at telling you which one you're about to do. You don't need new countertops. You might need to clean out the garage and fix the front door. Small stuff has a way of sticking in a buyer's head longer than it should, and the goal is to make sure nothing is quietly working against you when someone walks through. She'll tell you what's actually worth your time and money before you list.

Get It in Front of the Right People

A bad photo can kill interest in a perfectly good home. So can a listing that reads like it was written by a committee. Lisa puts real effort into how your home is presented and where it shows up — because there's no point in having great marketing that only reaches the wrong buyers. The goal is qualified people who are actually ready to move, not just curious scrollers.

When Offers Come In, Know What You're Looking At

Price is just one part of an offer. The terms, the contingencies, the timeline — all of it matters, and some of it can cost you more than a lower number would have. Lisa sits down with you, goes through everything clearly, and makes sure you're negotiating with a full picture instead of just reacting. That's usually where the real difference gets made.
