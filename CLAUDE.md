## 4. Audiences (two lanes, one brand)

**Lane 1 — PRIMARY (~80%): Spanish-speaking buyers in Elkhart County.** Ages 25–45, young families, first or second purchase, FHA/USDA buyers, seeking stability, nervous about the process, need step-by-step clarity, value trust and relationship. Positioning: la guía confiable que los acompaña paso a paso. Distinguishing qualities: Guía clara y paciente · Protectora de sus intereses · Puente cultural y profesional.

**Pain points → answers (drives all /es/ copy and FAQs):**
1. "No entiendo el proceso" → step-by-step education in Spanish, simplified documents
2. "Miedo a ser engañados" → total transparency, constant communication, protection at every decision
3. "No sé si califico" → trusted lender connections, credit plan, personalized guidance
4. "Estoy cansado de rentar" → rent-vs-buy comparisons, clear financial strategy
5. "Miedo a perder dinero" → local market analysis, data-driven advice

**Lane 2 — GROWTH: English-speaking sellers in Goshen/Elkhart.** Ages 40–65, 5–20 years in home, equity-rich, considering selling/downsizing, want strategy not just MLS placement. Positioning: bilingual strategist offering professional marketing and access to BOTH English- and Spanish-speaking buyer pools. Qualities: data-driven strategist · connector of two markets · modern professional with personal service. Pain points: timing, true home value, fear of sitting on market, stress, "why you?" (answer: 120+ transactions, bilingual reach, clear strategy).

Secondary audiences (phase 2+): SRES® senior transitions, relocators.

**Channel segmentation rule (from brand doc):** Spanish-dominant buyer education on personal/social profiles; English-dominant seller strategy on business page. Website carries both, fully bilingual.

## 7. Bilingual Rules (the moat)

- Spanish pages written natively for Spanish speakers (Mexican/Central American usage; "usted" register) — never machine-translated.
- Every EN page pairs with its ES page: visible EN | ES toggle, `hreflang` tags (en, es, x-default → EN).
- Spanish content targets Spanish search phrases ("agente de bienes raíces que habla español en Elkhart", "cómo comprar una casa en Indiana"), not translated keywords.
- Spanish pages are equal citizens: full schema, internal links, FAQs.
- **One language per page (language purity rule):** each page contains a single language, declared in its HTML lang attribute. Exceptions on EN pages: the "Hablo español" banner and the EN|ES toggle — nothing else. On ES pages: only the language toggle. The former Spanish CTA band on the EN homepage is replaced by the "Hablo español" banner once /es/ is live; its full invitation copy moves to /es/.
- /es/ copy is built from the Lane-1 pain-point library in section 4.

## 8. Page Standards (every build)

**Content:** answer the page's primary question in the first 1–2 sentences · one H1, logical H2/H3 · 5–10 original FAQs from real client questions · 5–10 contextual internal links · hyper-local references (neighborhoods, schools, parks, RV-industry employers, Old Bag Factory, Lerner Theatre, First Fridays, Wellfield Gardens, ZIPs, commutes) · write until fully answered, density over padding · voice per section 3.

**Technical:** title 50–60 chars (keyword + location + Lisa Collio) · meta description 145–160 chars (location + keyword + benefit + CTA) · canonical (https://lisacolliorealtor.com/... form) · hreflang pair · BreadcrumbList · schema per section 9 · descriptive image filenames + bilingual-appropriate alt text · WebP compression · mobile-first, Core Web Vitals green · locked footer signature · "Hablo español" banner (EN pages) / EN toggle (ES pages).

**Images:** Lisa's original photography ONLY (sold homes, client moments, Goshen/Elkhart photo walks, red-blazer professional imagery per brand doc). No stock or AI-generated property/people/place images.
