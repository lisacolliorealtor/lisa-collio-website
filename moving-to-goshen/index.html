Moving to Goshen, Indiana: What to Know Before You Move

Goshen is a city of roughly 34,000 people about 25 miles southeast of South Bend, and most people considering a move here are weighing the same three questions: what housing actually costs, where the work is, and whether daily life will fit. This page answers those directly — including the parts that aren't a sales pitch.

Lisa Collio has spent her career helping buyers and sellers in Goshen and Elkhart, Indiana, and the most useful thing she can offer someone who hasn't lived here yet isn't a list of amenities. It's context. Here's what she tells people who call from out of state.

Where Goshen sits, and what that means for getting around

Goshen is the county seat of Elkhart County, in the northern part of the state — the region locally called Michiana. Elkhart is 10 miles north; South Bend is about 25 miles northwest. South Bend International Airport, the nearest commercial service, is roughly 36 miles away; Fort Wayne International is about 61 miles. Goshen Municipal Airport, about 3.5 miles southeast of downtown, handles general aviation.

Goshen and Elkhart together form the Elkhart–Goshen Metropolitan Statistical Area, part of the larger South Bend–Elkhart–Mishawaka combined region — which matters more than it sounds, because it means the job market you're moving into is bigger than the city limits suggest.

The Indiana Toll Road (I-80/90) runs north of the city, so Chicago is about two and a half hours by car — worth noting that Goshen observes Eastern time and Chicago is on Central, which quietly buys you an hour on the way there and costs you one coming back. US-33 and State Road 15 carry most local traffic.

For most residents, the commute story is simple: it's short. That's one of the real differences people notice coming from a metro area, and it tends to change how the evening feels more than anything else on this page.

What daily life actually looks like here

Downtown Goshen is a compact business district with a dense mix of independent shops, restaurants, and coffee places — closely clustered rather than spread across a strip. First Fridays, a monthly downtown event, is the clearest window into it if you're visiting to decide. The Old Bag Factory, a historic industrial building converted into shops and working studios, anchors the north end. The Goshen Farmers Market runs year-round.

For time outdoors, the Pumpkinvine Nature Trail is the best-known asset — a paved rail-trail running northeast out of town toward Middlebury and Shipshewana. Fidler Pond Park and Abshire Park sit inside the city; Ox Bow Park is a short drive away along the Elkhart River.

Goshen College, a small liberal arts college known for its international programs, adds a layer most towns this size don't have: public lectures, concerts, and a steady flow of people from elsewhere.

Where people work

The regional economy has a center of gravity, and it's recreational vehicles. The Elkhart–Middlebury–Goshen corridor is commonly cited as producing the great majority of North America's RVs. Thor Industries is headquartered in Elkhart; Forest River, Keystone RV, Lippert Components, and Jayco all have major operations within a short drive. A significant share of buyers relocating to Goshen are coming for a job in that industry or its supply chain.

Beyond RVs, the largest local employers include IU Health Goshen Hospital, Goshen Community Schools, and Goshen College. Manufacturing outside the RV sector, healthcare, construction, and agriculture round out the picture. Plenty of residents also commute to Elkhart, Middlebury, Mishawaka, or South Bend without much difficulty.

Schools

Goshen is served by Goshen Community Schools, a district of nine schools with roughly 6,200 to 6,500 students. That includes Goshen High School — home of the RedHawks — plus Goshen Junior High, Goshen Intermediate, Chandler Innovation Academy, and five elementary schools. Private and parochial options exist in the area as well, and Goshen College serves the community at the post-secondary level.

A quirk worth knowing about before you get attached to an address: Goshen's boundary with Concord Community Schools cuts into the north side of the city, and that same district also reaches into southern Elkhart — Dunlap sits between the two cities and the school boundary doesn't follow the city line. A Goshen or Elkhart mailing address doesn't tell you the district on its own.

Bus service adds another wrinkle. It isn't guaranteed for every address even within an assigned district — it depends on the specific route, and routes shift year to year.

Lisa doesn't rank schools or steer anyone toward a particular attendance area, and no agent should. What she will do is confirm which boundary a given address actually falls in before you write an offer, and point you to the district's own enrollment office to confirm both the assignment and bus eligibility directly — that confirmation should always come from the district, not from an agent or a website.

The housing stock

Goshen's housing tells the story of when the city grew. Near the center you'll find late-1800s and early-1900s homes — a lot of character, and mechanicals, windows, and foundations that deserve a careful inspection. Move outward and you hit mid-century construction on larger lots. The edges of the city and the areas along the main corridors carry post-2000 subdivisions and current new construction. Outside the city limits, acreage and farm properties open up.

That variety is genuinely useful, because it means the same budget can buy very different things depending on what you prioritize. Two buyers with identical pre-approvals routinely end up in completely different parts of town.

What none of this tells you is price, because price moves. Current figures — median sale price, days on market, inventory — live on Lisa's market data page, sourced from the local MLS and updated quarterly. Anything you read about Goshen home prices in a relocation article without a date on it is worth ignoring.

What it costs to live here

Housing is the headline: it costs meaningfully less in Goshen than in most of the country, and less than in the metro areas many transplants are leaving. Utilities and commuting costs tend to run lower too, largely because the drives are shorter.

Indiana's property tax structure includes a homestead deduction and constitutional caps on the tax rate applied to owner-occupied homes, which makes annual carrying costs more predictable than in a number of other states. Rates and assessments vary by parcel, so confirm the specific number with the Elkhart County Assessor's office before you build it into a budget rather than relying on a rule of thumb.

Winter heating is the line item people from warmer states underestimate. Ask for a year of utility history on any home you're serious about — sellers can usually provide it.

The honest trade-offs

Every relocation guide has a "cons" section that lists nothing anyone would actually mind. Here are the real ones.

Public transportation is limited. The Interurban Trolley runs routes connecting Goshen to Elkhart and Dunlap, with connections onward toward Elkhart's Amtrak station and South Bend — but the stops are few, and Goshen is not a place you'd plan a daily life around transit. If you're coming from a city where you got by without a car, having one here will prove very useful. Budget for it.

Winter is a real season. Snow, gray stretches, and lake-effect systems rolling in off Lake Michigan. Some people love it. Some people spend three Februaries here and start looking south. You should know which one you are before you buy, not after.

The job market is concentrated. The RV industry's strength is also a form of exposure — it's cyclical, and when RV demand softens the region feels it. If your household income would depend entirely on that sector, that's a conversation worth having with a financial professional before you commit to a mortgage payment.

It's a small city. Fewer late-night options, fewer specialty retailers, a shorter list of restaurants than a metro area. Most residents find the trade worth making. Not everyone does.

Goshen or Elkhart?

People relocating to the area often arrive assuming they'll choose a city first and a house second. In practice it usually runs the other way. Elkhart is larger, closer to the Toll Road and to South Bend, and has its own downtown built around the Lerner Theatre and Wellfield Botanic Gardens. Goshen is smaller and denser at the center.

The practical advice: tour in both, and pay attention to the drive to wherever you'll actually be working. Twelve minutes versus thirty-five changes a life more than a countertop does. Lisa works throughout both cities and has no reason to push you toward either one. More on the second option is on the Elkhart community page.

One thing worth knowing about buying here from a distance

A buyer relocating for a supplier job once told Lisa, before ever seeing the area, that he wanted new construction and nothing older than ten years — no projects, no surprises. They toured new construction first, exactly as asked. Then, because they had an hour left, they walked through a 1920s house near downtown that he'd have scrolled past online.

He bought the 1920s house. Not because Lisa talked him into it, but because a photograph can't tell you what a room feels like at four in the afternoon in October. His original criteria weren't wrong — they were just built entirely out of things he could measure from another state.

That's most of what an agent is actually for when you're moving from somewhere else: making sure the list you arrive with gets tested against the place itself before it hardens into a decision. It's the same thinking behind The Next Chapter Method™ — start with the life you're building, and let the house serve it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is there to do in Goshen, Indiana?

More than a city this size suggests: a National Register historic district with independent retail and a restored 600-seat theater, a rail-trail running out toward Shipshewana, county parks along the Elkhart River, and a monthly downtown event that shuts the street down.

What community events happen in Goshen during the year?

The calendar runs year-round — First Fridays every month since 2007, the Farmers Market weekly, an annual air show at the municipal airport, and the Elkhart County 4-H Fair in late July, one of the largest county fairs in the country.
